When I started, I was writing a model to try to simulate accurately the 2024
USA presidential election with pre-election polling data. This could be
easily checked as the results are out, as well as those of many public models.
Once I finished with that, I grew interested in what would happen with more and
more parties. I eventually stumbled over the main problem statement - 
"What would be the optimal number of parties in this system such that 
voters are the most 'happy' in aggregate?". The model was easy to make from
there as I had many of the parts (including the vector concept and distance formulae)
already completed for my previous parts, and after some editing, I was able to find it.


[id="overview-findings"]  — Overview card: Key Findings (2–3 sentences)

The model returned an expected results with interesting footnotes. I hypothesized
before running the model that it would find some sort of sweet spot between the
2 party system (which bows to hard division and negative partisanship), and 5+
parties, which would cause fragmentation. But it was interesting that the 3 party
system not only proved improvement by over 0.1 length to party, but also was
one of the least noisy systems.
