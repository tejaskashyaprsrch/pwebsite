[id="sim3-desc"]  — claudeElectionModel.py description (2–3 paragraphs)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Write about the claudeElectionModel.py simulation here.
Parties are spread across the ideological spectrum with position noise epsilon.
Voters cluster near parties with some noise.
The metric is collective alienation — average voter distance to the winner.
Structure: 50 trials × 200 elections per trial × 2,000 voters per election.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[id="sim3-collective-alienation"]  — Define collective alienation (1 paragraph)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Write about what collective alienation means here.
It is the average Euclidean distance between each voter and the winning
party's position in 10-dimensional ideology space.
Why is this a meaningful measure of democratic quality?
